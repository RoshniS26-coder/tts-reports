0 · TL;DR

  • 7 fine-tunes of ai4bharat/indic-parler-tts. v6 was the best scraped-data baseline; v7 (mar-hin-betacraft-tts) reproduces it on copyright-safe synthetic data.
  • Key result: v7's Marathi intelligibility (Sarvam WER 2.6%) essentially matches the Gemini teacher (4.5%) — the synthetic distillation worked.
  • The lesson across versions: quality tracked data hygiene and speaker balance, not raw hours or steps (v2/v3 had the most data + steps yet sounded worst).
  • Two consistency fixes tried and rejected: FreeVC voice-conversion (flat/soft) and IndicF5 one-shot cloning (WER 85.5% — broken). Decoding at temp 0.7 remains the best available lever.

1 · Model selection — why Indic Parler-TTS

We chose ai4bharat/indic-parler-tts (0.9B, Apache-2.0): it natively supports Marathi & Hindi with named built-in speakers (Sunita, Divya…) and caption-driven style control ("expressive, warm, storytelling… very clear audio"), it is commercially licensable (unlike training on a proprietary API's output), and it fine-tunes on modest data. Architecture: autoregressive decoder over DAC audio-codec tokens, conditioned on a frozen Flan-T5 caption encoder; objective is cross-entropy (no mel/GAN loss). Baselines we compared against (below): the pretrained Indic-Parler, Meta MMS, and IndicF5.

3 · v7 — the copyright-safe synthetic pivot

Scraped audio (the v1–v6 data) is a legal risk for a commercial product. v7 regenerates everything synthetically: Gemini writes stories → Gemini-TTS "Sulafat" voices them → we fine-tune Parler on those clean pairs. Result: v7 wins on codebook-0 (2.768 vs v6 3.254) and matches CLAP, and — most importantly — its reliable Sarvam WER is excellent (§5). Full detail: the mar-hin-betacraft-tts technical report.

What I heard by ear (v7): the initial outputs had buzzing trails at sentence ends and voice-timbre drift on every sentence. The buzz was removed by lowering temperature to 0.7 (from the default 1.0) + a fixed seed + a trailing fade. The residual timbre drift is architectural (see §4).

4 · Post-training trials — fixing buzz & drift (with reasons)

4.1 Temperature / decoding sweep

Each sentence is generated independently; the default temp 1.0 gave buzz + drift. We swept the decoding settings. Finding: 0.7 is the stable optimum — anything that constrains the distribution further makes the model fail to stop (runaway buzzy tails):

4.2 Text sanitisation & trailing fade

A story title with markdown/dash (**राजू – …**) was fed verbatim to the model, which dropped "राजू" and hallucinated "बाघ". Fix: strip markup before synthesis. A 120 ms trailing raised-cosine fade then lets any residual end-of-sentence hiss decay smoothly instead of cutting off as a "buzz". Both are in the narration script.

4.3 FreeVC voice-conversion (Coqui) — rejected

Idea: post-process the Parler audio through a zero-shot voice converter (FreeVC + WavLM) to force every sentence onto one reference timbre, killing drift without retraining. Result: it did normalise the voice and even topped the signal metrics (STOI 0.998, PESQ 4.18, SI-SDR 28.1), but by ear it was flat and soft (UTMOS 2.61), and WER stayed at 20.0%. FreeVC's WavLM is English-biased, and a short reference gives a weak, averaged timbre. Verdict: consistent but lifeless → rejected.

4.4 IndicF5 one-shot cloning — rejected

Idea: IndicF5 (AI4Bharat's Indic F5-TTS) is Indic-native and clones a reference voice for every sentence — consistent by design. Result: a version mismatch between the installed f5_tts and the checkpoint left the GRN normalisation layers randomly initialised. The output sounds fluent (UTMOS 3.09 — the highest of any clip!) but is gibberish: WER 85.5%. A textbook case of naturalness ≠ intelligibility. Verdict: rejected in this configuration (a matching f5_tts pin would be the retry).

4.5 Measuring the voice drift (speaker-cosine consistency)

The sentence-to-sentence timbre drift — the project's #1 open problem — is quantified with speaker-embedding cosine consistency (Resemblyzer): windowed voiceprints within one story, averaged pairwise. Higher = steadier voice. The teacher is the ceiling; FreeVC (which forces one voice) nearly matches it — validating both the metric and that FreeVC really does fix drift.

Takeaway: FreeVC (0.754) ≈ teacher (0.758) proves post-hoc voice conversion fixes the drift — its cost is expressiveness (flat). Decoding barely moves consistency (temp 1→0.7: 0.72→0.738) — so the durable fix is a consistent-by-design model (a correctly-installed IndicF5) or a calmer-data retrain, not decoding.

7 · Infrastructure & effort (with reasons)

  • Training — RunPod GPU. v7 ran on an RTX A5000 (24 GB, $0.27/hr), chosen over an L4 ($0.39/hr) because the bf16 recipe was tuned for 24 GB and the A5000 is cheaper; 16 GB cards were rejected (no headroom / bf16). Run: 4 epochs, ~400 steps, ~27 min train, < $1. Earlier versions ran 1–2 h on similar 24 GB cards. Model + dataset pushed to a private HuggingFace repo the moment training ended (so a pod stop can't lose them).
  • Post-training inference — the bottleneck. Narration/experiments were attempted on an 8 GB MacBook Air, which proved too small: Parler generation ran ~1 hour per story, and heavier models (FreeVC, IndicF5) or long low-temp generations were OOM-killed. A separate, subtle failure: Coqui's model hosting (the company shut down) served truncated downloads that corrupted the FreeVC/ WavLM checkpoints — fixed by pulling verified copies from GitHub's release CDN. Takeaway: move VC / batch regeneration to a GPU.

8 · Conclusions & next steps

  • v7 (mar-hin-betacraft-tts) is a success on its core thesis: copyright-safe synthetic data matched the scraped-data baseline and delivered Marathi intelligibility (WER 2.6%) on par with the Gemini teacher (4.5%).
  • Ship setting: temperature 0.7 + fixed seed + text sanitiser + trailing fade.
  • Open problem: sentence-to-sentence timbre drift (architectural in Parler). Decoding can't fix it; FreeVC (flat) and IndicF5 (broken config) didn't win. Next: (a) retry IndicF5 with a matched f5_tts version — it's Indic-native and consistent-by-design, the most promising path; (b) strengthen Hindi with more data; (c) run VC/regeneration on a GPU, not the 8 GB Mac.
