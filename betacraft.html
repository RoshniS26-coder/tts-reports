1 · What this model is, and why it exists

Earlier project models (v1–v6) were trained on scraped / YouTube narration, which carries copyright risk for a commercial kids-content product. mar-hin-betacraft-tts replaces that data with a fully synthetic, copyright-safe corpus: original stories are written by Gemini, then voiced by Gemini-TTS Pro (voice "Sulafat") to produce clean (audio, text) pairs, on which we fine-tune the Apache-2.0 Indic Parler-TTS model. Expressiveness transfers through the audio; a single name-anchored caption selects the voice (Sunita = Marathi, Divya = Hindi). This is a knowledge-distillation setup: Gemini-Sulafat is the teacher, our Parler model is the student.

Treated as research / PoC until the Gemini Terms-of-Service "no competing model" clause is cleared or narration is commissioned. The base model (Indic Parler, Apache-2.0) is unaffected.

3 · Training & the RunPod configuration (and why)

Training is a manual accelerate loop over DAC audio-codec tokens (cross-entropy objective; no mel-spectrogram or GAN loss — those belong to other TTS architectures).

Why the RTX A5000 (24 GB): the config is bf16 and sized for 24 GB (effective batch 32 = batch 4 × grad-accum 8). We deliberately avoided 16 GB cards and T4/V100 (no native bf16 → OOM). The A5000 was chosen over an equivalent L4 because it is cheaper ($0.27 vs $0.39/hr) and is exactly what the recipe was tuned on. Recipe: 4 epochs · lr 1e-4 cosine · warmup 50 · bf16 · frozen Flan-T5 text encoder · save_steps 100000 (no mid-run checkpoints — avoids the transient 2× disk spike that crashed earlier runs; the final model auto-saves once at the end).

6 · Voice consistency & the trailing buzz — and how we fixed them

6.1 Sentence-to-sentence voice drift (measured)

Parler generates each sentence independently with no persistent speaker vector, so timbre can drift across a story. We quantify it with speaker-embedding cosine consistency (Resemblyzer): windowed voiceprints within one story, averaged pairwise — higher = steadier voice. The Gemini teacher is the stable ceiling; min-cos (the worst-matching pair) is the sharpest drift signal.

Voicemean-cos↑min-cos↑Read
Gemini-Sulafat teacher0.7580.597stable ceiling

betacraft — Hindi0.7220.498similar drift

temp 0.7 is measurably steadier than temp 1 (0.738 vs 0.72), and its worst-case pairs never fall as far. Decoding can only nudge this — the drift is architectural; the full research report covers the voice-conversion route that reaches teacher-level consistency.

6.2 The trailing buzz — removed by seed + temperature

Some sentences ended in a buzzy hiss (a sharp tail, ~95% of its energy above 4 kHz). Adjusting the sampling seed and lowering the temperature (1.0 → 0.7), together with a 120 ms trailing fade, removes it. Listen — the same final sentence, before vs after the fix:

8 · Honest limitations

  • Sentence-to-sentence timbre drift. Parler has no persistent speaker vector across independent sentence generations; the seed+temp fix reduces but does not eliminate it. This is architectural — decoding tuning cannot fully solve it (see the full research report for the FreeVC and IndicF5 attempts).
  • Hindi lags Marathi (WER 20% vs 3%) — the Hindi set is smaller (3.29 h vs 5.21 h) and Devanagari- Hindi Sarvam scoring is stricter here; Hindi is usable but not yet at Marathi's clarity.
  • Input text must be clean — markdown/dashes in a story title caused a mispronunciation ("राजू" → hallucinated "बाघ"); fixed by a text-sanitiser in the narration script.

9 · How to read the graphs (plain language)

Graph / termMeaning
train/loss, eval/lossHow wrong the model's audio-token predictions are on training vs held-out clips. Falls then flattens = learning; eval turning up = overfitting.
codebook 0–8The codec stores 1 s of audio as 9 stacked token layers. Codebook 0 = coarsest & most audible (watch it most); higher = fine detail.
CLAPText↔audio alignment (0–1, higher = better) — a semantic "does the audio match the words/style" proxy.
WER / CERWord / character error rate vs the known script (lower = clearer). Our "accuracy" number — the TTS analog of mAP in detection.
UTMOS / SQUIMPredicted human naturalness (UTMOS 1–5) and signal quality (STOI/PESQ/SI-SDR). Reference-free.
What we optimise vs a YOLO's mAP: in detection you optimise one metric (mAP) directly. In TTS we minimise a token-prediction loss during training, but a lower loss does not reliably mean better speech — so loss is the training signal, and the quality verdict comes from WER + UTMOS + human ears, computed after training.
